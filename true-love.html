True love leaves no traces July 7th 2016

True love leaves no traces,
and no drunk ghosts, nor dead branches
no curses to shake us
in the dawn
true love
leaves
no mumblings of
the promises
that it once enthused,
rather——
true love crumples in
upon itself
and vanishes
with no puff, or
true love separates,
its being
over a thousand miles, or
True love
just
expires
from the laughter, expires
laughing at itself
expiring.
True love
does not,
lend itself to silence
yet it feels not
compelled
to announce its immanence,
in the moment
of its departure
True love
does not,
remain here all morning,
coming down on
the sofa and,
while the rain continues
true love
does not listen
to the rain petering out
or to whatever
the fuck
the speakers might play

True love
does not
listen to Leyland Kirby, nor does it
hammer on
stark piano drones
and
True love
does not love to read you aloud
your Nietzche, and
True love does not love
to annotate your
Jean Genet
True love
does
not count itself in our final fumbling
waltzes,
our spilt wine, our fears and our shattered glasses
True love
holds no challenge to
parting kisses, or singular pearl like
tears.
True love
does not wait,
for this hour, before the sun has
fully risen——
True love
does not keep
a ledger, and so
true love
does not calculate,
the tremendous total of it's silence,
or the baffling number
of our
dreamy silent days

True love
leaves no traces,
no messages, and
no debris
All that
true love
leaves is its truth, and its
only truth,
is that it has left,
just as if
to restate that——
all along
it was sovereign
and all along
it was
free.
